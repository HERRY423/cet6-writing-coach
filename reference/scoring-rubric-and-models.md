# 评分细则 + 14 分范文（带评分点注释）

## 一、六级写作真实评分标准（阅卷组内部分档）

总分 15 分，按 4 档评定，再微调 ±1：

### 14 分档（顶尖：折算 130+）
- **切题**：完全切题，立意有深度
- **表达**：句式变化丰富（5 种以上：倒装、强调、分词、独立主格、形式主语等）
- **词汇**：精准、地道、有亮点（少量高级词用对）
- **结构**：段落清晰，过渡自然，论证有让步/反驳
- **错误**：极少语法错误，无低级搭配错误

### 11 分档（优秀：折算 110–125）
- 切题，立意清楚
- 句式有变化（3 种以上）
- 用词较准，偶有不够地道
- 结构完整
- 有少量小错（不影响理解）

### 8 分档（合格：折算 90–105）⭐ **大多数考生在这里**
- 基本切题
- 句式以主从复合句 + 简单句为主，缺变化
- 用词正确但平淡，重复多
- 结构完整但论证套路化（举例 → 因此重要）
- 有一些语法错误

### 5 分档（不及格：70 以下）
- 部分切题或跑题
- 大量简单句 / 病句
- 用词错误多
- 结构混乱或论证缺失

### 11→14 的关键跃迁（用户要冲分的核心）
1. **句式数量从 3 种 → 5 种以上**（重点练倒装、独立主格、抽象主语句）
2. **每段加一个让步 / 反驳**（"Critics may argue that ... however ..."）
3. **开头结尾抛弃 Nowadays / In conclusion**，用现象描写或隐喻
4. **替换 5 个最烂大街的词**（important / many / good / think / show）

## 二、14 分范文 × 4 篇（每段带 💡 给分点）

### 范文 1：议论文 — On the Value of Reading in the Digital Age

> Walk into any café or classroom today, and one sight repeats itself: heads bowed, eyes fixed on glowing screens. 💡【开头：现象描写代替 Nowadays，给评卷老师"这不是模板作文"的第一印象】Books, once our companions, have been quietly displaced by an endless scroll. Yet the case for reading — slow, deliberate, immersive — has never been more compelling.
>
> Reading, far from being a relic of a pre-digital era, cultivates faculties that no algorithm can replace. 💡【far from being … 让步结构 + cultivate faculties 精准搭配】It demands patience, the very capacity our attention-fractured age erodes. It rewards depth, forcing us to dwell with an idea rather than swipe past it. A 2024 study at Oxford found that students who read at least thirty minutes a day for a month showed measurable gains in empathy and critical reasoning — qualities that browsing cannot manufacture. 💡【数据论据，避免 Edison/Jobs 万能例子】
>
> Critics may counter that screens deliver the same information faster. 💡【让步段：11 → 14 的关键】They are not wrong about speed; they are wrong about substance. Information glanced at is rarely information retained. To read a book is to enter into a sustained conversation with another mind — a conversation a feed, by its very design, cannot offer.
>
> If knowledge is the compass and curiosity the wind, then deliberate reading remains the boat we choose to board. Without it, we do not so much navigate the modern world as drift through it. 💡【结尾隐喻 + 回扣开头，绝不用 In conclusion】

**得分点小结**：6 种句式（倒装、让步、形式主语、独立主格、强调、分词状语），4 处高级搭配，让步段独立成段，开头结尾呼应。

### 范文 2：图表作文 — The Rise of Online Learning (2015–2025)

> The figures tell a story almost too dramatic to be true: in just one decade, the share of Chinese university students engaged in online learning has surged from a modest 18 percent to a striking 76 percent. 💡【开头用数据反差，避免 The chart shows...】Behind this number lies a quiet revolution in how knowledge itself is acquired.
>
> Several forces have driven the trend. 💡【避免 firstly/secondly，直接抛因】The proliferation of affordable bandwidth has lowered the threshold of access; the pandemic, however regrettable, forced both institutions and students to confront the viability of remote instruction; and a generation raised on screens proved unusually adept at learning through them.
>
> Yet the surge is not without its discontents. 💡【转折让步】Online learning, for all its convenience, can dilute the discipline that a physical classroom imposes. Engagement without supervision becomes optional; passive viewing masquerades as active study. Whether this mode will, on balance, elevate education or hollow it out depends less on the technology than on how thoughtfully we wield it.

**得分点**：strong opening with data, 因果分析三层（technology / context / generation），独立主格，让步反驳，结尾上升到哲学层面。

### 范文 3：书信类 — A Letter Recommending a Book to a Friend

> Dear Wei,
>
> Your recent message mentioned that you have been feeling unmoored — uncertain about graduate plans, weary of the constant noise. It struck me that you might find unexpected solace in a book I finished last week: *Tuesdays with Morrie* by Mitch Albom. 💡【开头不寒暄，直接呼应对方处境】
>
> The book chronicles a young journalist's weekly visits to his dying mentor, a sociology professor named Morrie. 💡【清晰的内容介绍，但不剧透】What lifts it above the genre of "feel-good memoir" is its quiet refusal to offer easy answers. Morrie does not tell us how to live; he shows us how to attend — to people, to small joys, to the present moment that the future-obsessed so often miss.
>
> I am not suggesting the book will resolve your uncertainties; few books can. 💡【让步，避免推销腔】But I have a hunch that, like me, you will close its final chapter with a clearer sense of what truly warrants your worry — and what does not.
>
> Looking forward to hearing your thoughts over coffee soon.
>
> Yours,
> Lin

**得分点**：书信体注意亲密但克制的语气，避免 hope you are well 等寒暄模板，结尾自然约见。

### 范文 4：谚语类 — "Failure is the Mother of Success"

> The proverb sounds reassuring: stumble, and you shall rise wiser. 💡【拆解谚语，避免直接抄题】Yet anyone who has actually failed knows that comfort is not the same as truth. Failure may be the mother of success — but only if we choose to be its faithful student.
>
> Consider the case of Dyson, the British inventor whose name now adorns vacuum cleaners worldwide. 💡【具体例子，非 Edison】It took him 5,127 prototypes before one worked. The failures themselves taught him nothing; what taught him was his deliberate reflection on each one — what went wrong, what to adjust, what to abandon entirely.
>
> Without that reflective discipline, failure breeds only more failure. The cliché flatters us into believing that mistakes are inherently educational. They are not. A mistake unexamined is simply a wound that scars; a mistake interrogated becomes a lesson that lasts. 💡【对仗 + 抽象论证】
>
> The proverb, then, deserves an amendment: failure is the mother of success — but reflection is the midwife.

**得分点**：先质疑谚语再修正（避免无脑赞同），具体例子 + 抽象论证结合，对仗修辞，结尾改写谚语本身。

## 三、用这些范文的正确姿势（告诉用户）

1. **不要背全文** — 阅卷一眼就看出来，反而扣分
2. **背"骨架 + 替换词"** — 比如范文 1 的 "Walk into any X, and one sight repeats itself: ..." 可以套到几乎任何题目
3. **每篇挑 3 个最打动自己的句子**，仿写 5 遍，写到肌肉记忆
4. **特别注意**：每篇都有一个让步段（Critics may counter / Yet ... is not without its ...）—— 这是 11 → 14 的分水岭
