# 严谨逻辑 + 思辨能力（14 分作文的"看不见的底盘"）

**先说真话**：阅卷老师其实不会拿"逻辑学"工具去分析你的作文。但他**能凭直觉感受到**——这篇是"想清楚了写的"还是"边写边凑的"。
**你能不能进 14 分档，看的就是这个直觉的方向。**

这个文件分两大模块：
- **模块 A：严谨逻辑**——让你的推理不漏风（避免常见谬误）、因果链扎实、让步反驳真的能"反驳"
- **模块 B：思辨能力**——让你的观点不流于"对错二分"，能重新框定问题、识别价值张力

两个模块都给：**机制 → 例子 → 改写 → 配套句型**。

# 模块 A：严谨逻辑

## A.1 中国考生最爱犯的 12 种逻辑谬误（看到 = 扣分）

阅卷老师不会指名"你这是滑坡谬误"，但他读到的时候会皱眉——而印象分就这样跌一档。

### 1. 滑坡谬误（Slippery Slope）— 出现率 ★★★★★

**症状**：从一个温和前提滑到一个夸张结论，中间步骤被省略。

> ❌ If we allow students to use smartphones in class, soon they will stop reading entirely, **and education itself will collapse**.

**为什么扣分**：从"上课用手机"到"教育崩塌"，中间有 5 个跳跃。
**怎么改**：要么承认中间步骤不必然发生，要么补出每一步的机制。

> ✅ Permitting smartphones in class **risks normalizing divided attention** — a habit that, **once formed**, becomes hard to switch off in contexts where deep focus matters.

### 2. 稻草人谬误（Straw Man）— ★★★★

**症状**：歪曲对方观点，然后攻击这个歪曲版。

> ❌ Critics of online learning think students should never use computers. **This view is absurd.**

（真实的批评者从没说"永远不用电脑"，你在攻击一个不存在的立场。）

> ✅ Critics of online learning argue, **more modestly**, that screen-based instruction erodes certain forms of sustained engagement. This concern deserves a serious response, not dismissal.

### 3. 二分谬误（False Dichotomy）— ★★★★★

**症状**：把复杂问题简化成"非黑即白、非 A 即 B"。

> ❌ Either we embrace AI entirely, or we fall behind.
> ❌ Young people today either lie flat or burn out.

> ✅ The choice is not between embracing AI entirely and rejecting it outright; it lies in **deciding which tasks we delegate and which we keep**.
> ✅ The space between lying flat and burning out is, in fact, where most thoughtful young people now live.

**高分句型**：
- `To frame the choice as [A or B] is to have answered it badly.`
- `The interesting question is not whether X, but in what form and on what terms.`

### 4. 以偏概全（Hasty Generalization）— ★★★★

**症状**：用一两个例子推出普遍结论。

> ❌ My roommate failed his exam because he played video games. **This proves that video games destroy academic performance.**

> ✅ My roommate's case **alone proves little**; but it does illustrate **one of several pathways** by which unstructured leisure can crowd out the consolidation work academic learning requires.

### 5. 因果倒置 / 相关 ≠ 因果（Correlation ≠ Causation）— ★★★★

**症状**：看到两件事一起发生，就断定其中一个导致另一个。

> ❌ Students who read more get better grades. **Therefore, reading causes academic success.**
（也许是擅长学习的学生才会主动读书；也许两者都受第三个因素影响。）

> ✅ Reading and academic success are tightly correlated. **Whether one causes the other, or both arise from a deeper trait — curiosity, perhaps, or sustained attention — is harder to pin down.** What seems clear is that the habit, once formed, reinforces itself.

### 6. 循环论证（Circular Reasoning）— ★★★

**症状**：结论本身就是前提的一部分。

> ❌ Reading is valuable because it has great value to those who read.
> ❌ This policy is right because the right course of action is to adopt it.

> ✅ 用具体内容支撑，不要回到"X is good because X is good"。

### 7. 诉诸权威 / 群众（Appeal to Authority / Popularity）— ★★★

> ❌ As Steve Jobs once said, "..." Therefore, ...
> ❌ Everyone agrees that ..., so it must be true.

**问题**：名人也会错；多数人也会错。
**怎么改**：引用之后必须**给理由**，不能让引用本身充当论据。

> ✅ Sontag once observed that "a library is a tool for shaping the future." **The observation gains its force not from her authority but from a deeper truth: every choice of what to preserve is, by implication, a choice of what kind of future we wish to inhabit.**

### 8. 偷换概念（Equivocation）— ★★★★

**症状**：同一个词在论证不同部分意思悄悄变了。

> ❌ Freedom is a fundamental human right. **Therefore, students should have the freedom to skip class.**
（前一个 "freedom" 是政治权利，后一个变成了"做任何想做的事的许可"。）

> ✅ 写之前**圈出关键概念**，确保全文用同一个意思。

### 9. 诉诸情感（Appeal to Emotion）— ★★

**症状**：用情绪化语言代替论证。

> ❌ How can anyone be so heartless as to oppose this policy?

议论文里你可以**唤起**情感，但不能**用**情感**代替**论证。

### 10. 红鲱鱼（Red Herring）— ★★★

**症状**：转移话题以回避真问题。

> ❌ 题目问"大学是否该重视通识教育"——你写了三段关于"中国大学生很努力"。

**自检**：每段写完问一句"这段在直接回应题目吗？"

### 11. 错误类比（False Analogy）— ★★★

**症状**：两件事在表面像，但在论证所需的关键维度上不同。

> ❌ A country is like a family; therefore, citizens should obey the government as children obey parents.
（国家和家庭在"成员是否能选择加入、是否能离开、规模、权力关系"等关键维度都不同。）

> ✅ 类比可以用，但**说清楚类比成立在哪个维度、在哪个维度失效**。

### 12. 过度限定 / 不当条件（Special Pleading）— ★★

**症状**：给自己的观点开例外、给对方的观点设苛刻条件。

> ❌ My evidence counts even though it's anecdotal, but your evidence doesn't count because it's also anecdotal.

## A.2 三种推理结构（明确知道自己在用哪一种）

### 演绎推理（Deductive）— 从一般到具体
> 前提：所有需要持续注意力的活动都被短视频削弱。
> 前提：深度阅读需要持续注意力。
> 结论：深度阅读被短视频削弱。

**用法**：可靠但常被认为"太理论"。**议论文里少用纯演绎**，因为它依赖前提百分百成立。

### 归纳推理（Inductive）— 从具体到一般
> 观察 1: 我自己读屏后回忆比读纸差。
> 观察 2: Oxford 研究显示纸质阅读者理解力更强。
> 观察 3: 同学普遍反映上网课后注意力下降。
> 结论（**概率性**）: 屏幕阅读可能削弱深度理解。

**用法**：议论文 80% 用归纳。**关键**：用 "suggest / appear to / likely / tend to" 而非 "prove / always"——保留可错性是思辨成熟的标志。

### 类比推理（Analogical）— 从一个领域到另一个
> 印刷术问世时，柏拉图担心写作会削弱记忆；今天我们担心 AI 会削弱思考。
> **类比有效维度**：每一次外化认知的技术都引发同样的焦虑。
> **类比失效维度**：但 AI 的速度和范围与印刷术不可同日而语，**所以我们不能用历史经验完全打消今天的忧虑**。

**用法**：议论文**第二段最爱用**，但必须主动说明类比的边界——否则就是 A.1.11 错误类比。

## A.3 因果链怎么搭得不松散

中国考生最常见的因果问题：**因果跳跃 + 唯一原因谬误**。

### 病例 1：跳跃式因果
> ❌ The Internet makes us lonely.
（中间机制呢？）

### 病例 2：唯一原因
> ❌ Young people lie flat because they are lazy.
（只给一个原因，等于宣告自己没想过。）

### 健康因果链的"三步搭法"

**步骤 1：声明因果关系（带强度限定）**
> Online interaction appears to contribute to, **though not single-handedly cause**, the rise in reported loneliness.

**步骤 2：给出机制（"X 通过什么导致 Y"）**
> The mechanism is subtler than the headlines suggest: it is not screen time as such, but **the substitution of frictionless, low-stakes contact for the higher-cost, higher-reward exchanges that historically built belonging**.

**步骤 3：承认替代解释**
> Other factors — urban anonymity, declining religious affiliation, longer working hours — almost certainly compound the effect. **The Internet is one strand in a thicker rope.**

**威力**：三步走完，你的因果论证从"高中生水平"到"研究生水平"，阅卷老师立刻感知到差别。

## A.4 让步反驳的真正写法（不是套模板）

90% 的考生让步段长这样：

> ❌ Some people may think that X. However, I still think Y is better because ...

**问题**：根本不算让步——你只是说了句"有人不同意"，然后立刻回到原观点，**完全没有真的考虑反方**。
阅卷老师一眼看穿。

### 真正有力的让步段：三步法

**步骤 1：把对方论点说到最强（钢人论证 Steel-manning）**
不要弱化、不要歪曲。**用对方会用的最强表述。**

**步骤 2：承认它的部分合理性**
不是 "However, ..." 一句话带过，而是真的说"这观点的力量在哪"。

**步骤 3：指出它的边界 / 不完整 / 隐藏成本**
不是说它"错"，而是说它"不完整"或"在某条件下失效"。

### 弱让步 vs 强让步（同一个观点对比）

**❌ 弱让步**：
> Some critics argue that online learning is harmful. However, online learning is actually beneficial because it is convenient and accessible.

**✅ 强让步**：
> Critics of online learning **make a serious point**: replacing the embodied classroom with a video feed strips away the informal exchanges — the question asked between sessions, the conversation in the corridor — that often matter as much as the lecture itself. **This loss is real, and any honest defense of online learning must own it.** What such criticism understates, however, is that the embodied classroom was, for many students, never truly accessible — to those at distant institutions, those with caregiving obligations, those whose disabilities the lecture hall accommodated only awkwardly. **Online learning does not merely substitute for the seminar; for many, it substitutes for nothing at all.** The trade-off, then, is not between perfect classrooms and inferior screens, but between an imperfect classroom available to some and an imperfect alternative available to many more.

读完这段，阅卷老师会想："这考生真的想过这个问题。"——这就是 14 分。

### 让步段的高级连接词梯度

| 强度 | 连接词 |
|------|--------|
| 引入对方观点 | Critics may counter that ... / It is sometimes argued that ... / A common objection holds that ... |
| 承认其力量 | The concern is not unfounded; ... / There is force in this objection: ... / They are not wrong about X — they are right that ... |
| 转折 | Yet what such criticism understates is ... / The objection nonetheless misreads ... / This much is true; what follows from it, however, is less clear ... |

# 模块 B：思辨能力

## B.1 重新框定问题（Reframing）—— 思辨的最高表现

**8 分考生**：被题目给出的框架牵着走，回答"是 / 不是"。
**14 分考生**：先质疑这个框架本身，给出"问题问错了"或"问题需要重问"。

### 四种重新框定的招式

#### 招式 1：把"是否" 改成 "在何种条件下 / 以何种形式"
- 题目：Are smartphones harmful to students?
- 8 分回答：Yes / No / Both.
- 14 分回答：**The harm is conditional on use patterns rather than on the device itself. The interesting question is not whether smartphones are harmful, but under what conditions they cease to be merely tools and start being habits.**

#### 招式 2：把"X vs Y" 改成 "我们误以为是 X vs Y，其实真问题是 Z"
- 题目：Should we read paper books or e-books?
- 8 分回答：两边各有好处。
- 14 分回答：**The paper-versus-screen debate distracts from the deeper question, which is not which medium we use but whether we still possess the attentional stamina to use either well.**

#### 招式 3：拒绝伪两难
- 题目：Should young people pursue dreams or stability?
- 8 分回答：要平衡。
- 14 分回答：**The framing assumes dreams and stability are rival categories. They are not. The most sustaining dreams are those built atop stable enough foundations to permit risk; the most worthwhile stability is that which leaves room for dreams.**

#### 招式 4：把"问题"重新归因
- 题目：Why are young people unhappy?
- 8 分回答：因为压力大。
- 14 分回答：**To attribute the unhappiness of young people to "pressure" alone is to mistake a symptom for a cause. The deeper source may be the loss of plausible scripts for a meaningful life — scripts which earlier generations could borrow uncritically and which ours must build from scratch.**

### 重新框定的高分句型库

> 1. `To frame the question as [A or B] is to have answered it badly: the more interesting question is [C].`
> 2. `What this debate ultimately turns on is not [常见焦点], but [真正焦点].`
> 3. `The temptation is to treat X as the problem. It may instead be the symptom of something deeper.`
> 4. `Before we can answer whether X, we have to ask whether X is even the right question.`
> 5. `The question is less whether X, than under what conditions X.`

## B.2 价值张力分析（Value Tension）—— 真实世界的复杂性

**普通考生**：把所有论点对齐到"好 / 坏"二分。
**思辨考生**：识别同一个事物里**两种都合理但相互拉扯的价值**。

### 经典价值张力（议论文常考）

| 张力 | 议题示例 |
|------|---------|
| 自由 vs 安全 | 网络监管、个人数据 |
| 效率 vs 公平 | 教育资源分配、AI 自动化 |
| 个人 vs 集体 | 防疫、共同富裕 |
| 进步 vs 传承 | 传统文化、城市更新 |
| 真实 vs 善意 | 朋友间是否要直言不讳 |
| 短期 vs 长期 | 环境政策、经济刺激 |
| 深度 vs 广度 | 教育、阅读、社交 |

### 怎么用价值张力写出深度

**示范**（题目：是否应该普及 AI 进入教育？）：

> The case for AI in education is not really a single case but two cases pulled in opposite directions. **The first prizes accessibility**: AI tutors can reach students whom human teachers cannot, at costs that put quality instruction within reach of more families than ever. **The second prizes formation**: an education is not only the transfer of skills but the imprint of a particular teacher's example — a thing AI, by its nature, cannot offer. **The honest answer is not that one side is right and the other wrong, but that any defensible policy must purchase one value at some cost to the other, and ought to do so openly.**

读完这段，阅卷老师会判断："这不是一个会写英语的考生，这是一个会思考的人。"——这一段单独能把分数拉一档。

## B.3 概念分层（Conceptual Layering）—— 把模糊的大词拆开

**症状**：考生满篇用 "freedom / success / happiness / education / progress" 等大词，但从不定义它们。
**结果**：论证浮在概念表面。

### 怎么做：把大词拆成 2-3 层

#### 例 1：拆 "Success"
- 表层：external markers — wealth, titles, social recognition
- 中层：competence — actually being good at what one does
- 深层：alignment — doing what one finds worth doing, regardless of recognition

> Most discussions of success conflate these three. A young person who has accumulated external markers without competence is famous but hollow; one who has competence without alignment is excellent but unhappy; only the rare convergence of all three constitutes what we usually mean, but rarely articulate, by a successful life.

#### 例 2：拆 "Freedom"
- 消极自由：免于干涉
- 积极自由：有能力做出有意义的选择
- 关系自由：在与他人的承诺中保有自主

> When critics decry restrictions on online speech, they typically invoke freedom in the first sense. When defenders point to the harm misinformation does, they implicitly invoke freedom in the second — the freedom of citizens to access accurate information needed for informed choice. The disagreement is not really about whether freedom matters, but about which freedom matters more here.

**威力**：这种分层一旦出现，你的作文立刻**站到 90% 同分段考生上面**。

## B.4 钢人论证（Steel-manning）—— 思辨成熟度的最高标志

不是攻击对方最弱的版本，而是**主动把对方论点说到最强，然后再回应**。

**对比**：

❌ **稻草人**：
> Some say AI will replace teachers. **This is ridiculous because no machine can show love.**

✅ **钢人**：
> The strongest version of the "AI will replace teachers" claim is **not that machines will mimic love**, but that the marginal cost of an AI tutor approaches zero, while a human teacher's time is irreducibly finite. **If learning outcomes can be made even 80 percent as good at 1 percent of the cost, the economic logic is brutal — and dismissing it on grounds of "but love" risks consigning AI tutors to the affluent while the rest get nothing.** The reply must therefore engage the economics, not evade them.

**练习方法**：每次写到反方观点，**先停三十秒**，问自己"对方最聪明的版本怎么说？"。
**长期效果**：你的论证会自动获得阅卷老师识别得到的"成熟感"。

## B.5 第三方视角（The Third Voice）

**症状**：考生只在 "I" 和 "they (critics)" 两个视角里切换。
**升级**：引入"第三种声音"——把问题放到更大的语境里。

### 三种"第三方"

#### 1. 历史视角
> A century from now, our debates about AI will look much as our debates about industrialization look to us — partly prescient, partly absurd, mostly worried about the wrong things.

#### 2. 跨文化视角
> The Anglophone debate frames online privacy as an individual right; the East Asian framing more often treats it as a relational obligation. Neither framing exhausts the matter; each illuminates what the other obscures.

#### 3. 未来一代视角
> The choices we make today about screen time will be inherited by children who never knew an alternative. Whether they thank us depends less on what we decide than on how honestly we wrestled with the decision.

# 模块 C：实战 —— 浅论证 vs 深论证 同段对比

题目：**Should social media be regulated?**

## ❌ 浅论证（8 分版）

> Social media is a big problem nowadays. **Many people** spend too much time on it and become **unhappy**. For example, my friend always checks her phone and **feels bad** when she sees others' lives. So **the government should regulate it** to protect young people. In conclusion, social media is **harmful** and **must be controlled**.

**诊断**：
- 谬误：以偏概全（1 个朋友 → 所有人）、二分（要么放任要么管制）
- 论证：无机制、无让步、无概念定义
- 思辨：0

## ✅ 深论证（14 分版）

> **The case for regulating social media is rarely as straightforward as either its critics or its defenders pretend.** Defenders rightly point out that the heaviest costs of regulation tend to fall on the smallest platforms — entrenching, not constraining, the dominant players. **This is a serious objection.** But it understates how the harms in question — eroded attention, intensified comparison, the algorithmic amplification of outrage — are not incidental to the platforms but **structurally produced** by an attention-extracting business model. **The honest framing is therefore neither "regulate" nor "leave alone," but: which design choices, which scale of platform, and which forms of harm warrant which kinds of intervention?** A century from now, our generation may be judged less by whether we regulated social media than by whether we managed to think about it with anything like the seriousness its hold on daily life deserves.

**这段示范了什么**：
- 让步（钢人）：先把"反对管制"的最强论点说出来
- 因果机制：把"刷手机"上升到"商业模式"层面
- 重新框定：把"是否管制"改成"何种设计、何种规模、何种伤害"
- 第三方视角（未来）：用一代人的回望收尾
- 概念分层：harms 不是单一的，至少三种

# 模块 D：思辨 + 逻辑的"自检八问"（写完通读必做）

考前 2 周开始练，逐句过你的作文：

1. **我的论点里有没有"非黑即白"？** —— 改成"在何种条件下"。
2. **我有没有把对方说成傻瓜？** —— 用钢人版本重写让步段。
3. **我的因果链中间有没有断裂？** —— 给出机制。
4. **我用了大词（freedom/success/education）但没定义？** —— 拆 2-3 层。
5. **我的例子能不能推出我的结论？** —— 别以偏概全。
6. **我有没有重新框定问题？** —— 至少一处。
7. **我有没有承认替代解释？** —— 一句话也行：other factors compound this。
8. **如果一个阅卷老师不同意我的观点，他读完会觉得"她至少认真考虑过我了"吗？** —— 这是 14 分的真实标准。

**8 个全部 yes，你这篇作文逻辑底盘就过关了。**

# 最后一句铁律

> **逻辑和思辨不能背，只能练。**
> 你可以一周记住 50 个高级词，但思辨能力**只能靠"每天就一个真题，写完后用上面 8 问逼自己重写"建立**。
> 14 分作文的真正秘密，不是英语水平更高，而是**写作时多想了 5 分钟、删了 30% 的废话、加了 1 处真正的让步**。
